Margaret Hamilton

  • Birth-Death: December 9, 1902 to May 16, 1985
  • Lived: Cleveland, Ohio
  • What she did: In 1961, she led the Software Engineering Division of MIT. She created the term "Software Engineer".
  • Impact:She had an impact on the world because she helped in the flight software for NASA's Apollo program which landed people on the moon for he first time. She is responsible for the software that allowed Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin on the moon.
